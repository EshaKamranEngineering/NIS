How the Non-Intrusive Sensor works

We simplified the process into four steps so a customer can quickly understand the system.

Step-by-step process

Current flows through a conductor.
Whenever electricity moves through a wire, it creates an electromagnetic field around that conductor.
The NIS detects the field externally.
The sensor is positioned around or beside the conductor so it can sense the field without cutting into the circuit.
The signal is processed.
The detected field is converted into an electrical signal that can be interpreted, calibrated, and prepared for monitoring.
Data becomes useful information.
The processed output is sent to a display, controller, or monitoring platform so users can track performance in real time.

Why “non-intrusive” matters

The key idea is that the sensor gets the information it needs from outside the wire. That makes the system easier to install and safer to use than methods that require breaking into the circuit.

In plain language

Think of it like checking activity in a room by listening through the wall instead of tearing the wall open. The NIS observes the electrical effects from the outside and turns them into data.

What users need to remember

  • No invasive connection is required for sensing.
  • The installation process can be simpler and faster.
  • The result is readable data for monitoring and decision-making.
